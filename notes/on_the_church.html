The Church
    Executive Summary

    The church is an organism made up of the people God has called to Christ.  Christ is the head, and the church submits to him.  The church is like a building, made up of believers as living stones, with Christ, the apostles, and the prophets as the foundation.

    Fellowship in the church is spiritual, not psychological, social or selfish. Therefore, church should be seen as a place for us to minister to each other - through  teaching, exhortation, encouragement, etc.

    Other Implications

    We are members.  The Bible was written primarily for the church.  The vast majority of the New Testament books were written to or about churches, rather than individuals. The Bible is almost useless for the "Lone Ranger Christian".  Abiding in Christ includes a healthy desire for fellowship in a church.

    No partiality. Christ builds the church with finely detailed attention to each member. We're here for specific reasons, with the goal of building up the church (Ephesians 4:29).  Members we don't like are more important to the church than we know, and in some cases we may be questioning Christ's judgement...

    Evangelism. The church began reaching out to all people almost from the start.  Evangelists preached using the figures and terms of the cultures they worked in - compare Acts 7 and Hebrews (to Jews), Romans (to Romans), Galatians (to Celts), and Acts 17 (to Greeks).

    Be on guard!  The head is Christ -- therefore, every teacher's teachings are subject to Christ. It is every member's responsibility to evaluate all teachings in the church as best as possible.  All teachings are measured against the Bible & the gospel. (Drew Berkemeyer's litmus test: "the answer to everything is: Jesus Christ")

    Mature yourselves/become holy. 2 Pe 1:5; 1 Thess 4:3; Eph 4:17; Col 3:5; Phil 1:27; Rom 13:14
    Build up one another. Rom 14:19, 15:2 et al
    Pray. Col 4:2; James 5:13-15; Phil 4:6; Rom 12:12, 15:30-31
    Rejoice always. 1 Thess 5:16; James 1:2; Phil 3:1, 4:4; Rom 12:12
    Speak to one another in psalms, hymns, and spiritual songs. Eph 5:19
    Requests, prayers, intercessions, and thanks be offered on behalf of all people. 1 Tim 2:1,2,8; 1 Thess 5:17-18; 2 Thess 3:1, Col 4:3
    Foster harmony. 1 Pe 3:8, 4:9; 2 Co 13:11; 5:12-13; Rom 12:16

    Stay alert, stand firm in the faith, show courage, be strong. 1 Co 16:13; 1 Pe 5:8; Col 2:7; Gal 5:1

    ( immorality: 1 Co 10:8; Eph 5:3; Rom 6:12-13; idolatry: 1 Co 10:7; 1 Jn 5:21 )
    Don't let anybody deceive you. Eph 5:6 et al
    Don't put Christ to the test. 1 Co 10:9; 1 Pe 2:16
    Don't complain. 1 Co 10:10; Phil 2:14
    Don't be divided. 1 Co 11:17-22
    Don't become conceited, provoking one another, being jealous of one another.  Gal 5:26; James 4:11; Rom 12:16
    Don't show prejudice. James 2:1
    Don't bother with foolish controversies, genealogies, quarrels, and fights about the law. Titus 3:9, Timothy 1:3-4,6-7  et al.
